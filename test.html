Summary

Run by the Indy Parks Department, this is a pretty nice place to play. While there aren’t dedicated pickleball courts (tennis courts that are used as pickleball courts as well), it’s not that big of a deal. Most people are used to playing on a tennis court with pickleball lines on it.

So, you have to show up to open play or not play at all. And if you do show up to open play, make sure you’ve checked the TeamReach group for Ellenberger to make sure it’s still happening that night. If they don’t have a volunteer then they usually won’t play.

Best Feature

Probably the most consistent and high turnout outdoor place to play. The play may not be too advanced but people show up. And it’s organized by 2 volunteers who are out there and make sure the courts stay rotating, people are playing in the right level, etc.

Courts are broken down into beginner, intermediate, and advanced. So, you can play at your level consistently as long as you’re not looking for 4.5 and higher games. While those types of players may go out there sometimes, it is rare.
